--Main Series--

The Elder Scrolls I: Arena

The Elder Scrolls I: Arena came out in 1994. The game takes place all over Tamriel during 889-890 of the 3rd Era. You play as a prisoner named Talin, who is tasked with saving the emperor, Uriel Septim VII from an evil battlemage named Jagar Tharn. Jagar Tharn trapped the emperor in another dimension and in order to get him out you must collect the pieces of the Staff of Chaos.

You'll notice that starting off as a prisoner in these games is a common theme.



The Elder Scrolls II: Daggerfall

The Elder Scrolls II: Daggerfall came out in 1996. The game takes place in parts of High Rock and Hammerfell during 405 of the 3rd Era. You play as The Agent or the Hero of Daggerfall, and are tasked by the emperor to put the spirit of King Lysandus, the King of Daggerfall, to rest as he was murdered, and to find a letter that sent he sent that went missing. You discover that Lysandus was betrayed and murdered and you put his killer to justice and put Lysandus's spirit to rest. You find the letter had ended up in the wrong persons hand and that emperor was trying to find the Mantella, the key to activting the Numidium. Once you obtain the Mantella, there are few different option on who to give it to. Regardless of who you give it to, once the Numidium is activated, it causes an anomaly known as the Warp in the West, and because of this all choices that are possible on who to give the Mentella to, happen simultaneously. This is known as Dragon Break.



The Elder Scrolls III: Morrowind

The Elder Scrolls III: Morrowind came out in 2002. The game takes place in Vvardenfell, in the province of Morrowind during 427 of the 3rd Era. You begin as a prisoner (told you) with orders from Emperor Uriel Septim VII. Soon after arriving to Vvardenfell, you find out about the prophecy of the Nerevarine, the reincarnation of the Dunmer hero Indoril Nerevar. Eventually you discover you are the Nerevarine and must fulfill a bunch of ancient prophecies. You rise the ranks of the three Great Houses and gain the trust of the Ashlander Tribes. After this you discover a secret about the Tribunal and how they betrayed the Nerevar. At the end of the storyline you face off against Dagoth-Ur, a friend of the Nerevar who became an enemy. Wilst all of that was happening, Dagoth-Ur was using the Heart of Lorkhan to become a god and release the Blight to mutate and corrupt life.

While the Nerevarine did defeat Dagoth-Ur, his story did not end there as two DLC's were release for Morrowind, Tribunal and Bloodmoon. Tribunal is set in in same year as the main events of Morrowind, but it centralizes in Mournhold, the capital city of Morrowind. The big story of Tribunal is Amalexia, one of the three mortal god-kings of Morrowind, is becoming power hungry and wants to keep her grasp on her people so she orchestrates different crisis in Mournhold to maintain her image. At the end, Amalexia goes full villain and sends you to Sotha Sil's Clockwork City where you find Sotha Sil dead and face off against Amalexia, ending the Tribunal DLC. The Bloodmoon DLC is also in the same year, but takes place on the island of Solstheim. The story for this DLC is that the East Empire Company wants help establishing a new colony called Raven Rock where you can help build, defend, and deal with polotics. However, there is something else going on. The daedric prince Hircine is about to start something called the Bloodmoon Prophecy, hence the name. The prophecy involves Hircine's big hunt, where he and many werewolves hunt selected prey. At the end you face off against Hircine and choose an aspect of his to fight.



The Elder Scrolls IV: Oblivion

The Elder Scrolls IV: Oblivion came out in 2006. The game takes place in the province of Cyrodiil during 433 of the 3rd Era. You begin as a prisoner (again) and Emperor Uriel Septim VII approaches your cell with his personal guard and he declares that he has seen you in dreams, like a prophecy. The emperor has you accompany him and his guards through a secret passage in your cell where he is soon assassinated by the Mythic Dawn, a daedric cult who worships Mehrunes Dagon. Before his death, the emperor tells you about a secret heir he has named Martin and you must find him. You find Martin in the city of Kvatch while it is being attacked by daedra through oblivion gates. This is now where the player earns the title the Hero of Kvatch. You take Martin to the headquarters of the blades to keep him safe. After this you recover the Amulent of Kings, ancient armor of Talos, and rebuild the Blades. The city of Bruma is then attacked by daedra and you must help defend it. Martin then asks for the Hero of Kvatch's help to perform a ritual to create a new artifact of Akatosh. Mehrunes Dagon then attacks the Imperial City himself and Martin saccrifces himself, turning himslef into a giant flaming dragon to defeat Mehrunes Dagon and send him back to his plane of oblivion.

Just like Morrowind though, Oblivion got some DLC content, the Knights of the Nine and the Shivering Isles. The Knights of the Nine DLC is about an ancient Ayleid king, Umaril the Unfeathered, returning from the dead. Umaril has his warriors killing priests of the Nine Divines and in the Hero of Kvatchs quest to stop Umaril, they reform the Knights of the Nine. Eventually you find Umaril in his fortress and you kill him and then use a holy artifact to follow him to the spiritual plane and kill him their as well. The Shivering Isles DLC takes place in the Shivering Isles, the daedric prince of madness Sheogoraths plane of oblivion. A portal appears in Niben Bay and you go to the Shivering Isles where Sheogorath warns you about an incoming event called the Greymarch, in which Jyggalag, the daedric prince of order, destroys the Shivering Isles. Sheogorath asks the Hero of Kvatch to help prevent the Greymarch. In this process you help the lands of Mania and Dementia and discover that Sheogorath disappears and the Greymarch beings and you take Sheogorath's place as the prince of madness. You eventually find out that Sheogorath and Jyggalag are the same person. Jyggalag was cursed by the other daedra to become Sheogorath. Essentially Sheogorath is stuck in a cycle of turning into Jyggalag and destroying his own realm and repeating the process. After you take Sheogoraths place, you fight Jyggalag and defeat him breaking the curse and Jyggalag is now free to roam the planes of oblivion. The Hero of Kvatch then is crowned the new Sheogorath and actually shows up in the next Elder Scrolls game.

The Elder Scrolls IV was just remastered, like it was released April 22. This remaster enhances graphics and changes a couple gameplay mechanics, as well as comes with the DLCs included.



The Elder Scrolls V: Skyrim

The Elder Scrolls V: Skyrim was released in 2011. The game takes place in the province of Skyrim during 201 of the 4th Era. You begin as a prisoner (figures) being transported to Helgen for execution along side about a dozen stormcloak soldiers and Ulfric Stormcloak. Right before the headsmans axe hits your neck, the town is attacked by a dragon. After you escape Helgen, you make your way to Riverwood then to Whiterun where the court wizard asks you to retrieve a stone tablet from an ancient nord barrow. Once you return with the tablet the Jarl gets news that there is a dragon attacking one of their watchtowers and asks you to help fight. You go with a couple of city guards and fight and kill the dragon. Something weird happens though and the guards believe that you devooured the dragons soul and that makes you Dragonborn, a person blessed with blood of the dragons and the natural gift of the Thu'um, dragon shouts. The Dragonborn is then summoned by the Greybeards, a group of men dedicated to peace and mastery of the Thu'um. The Greybeards train the dragonborn and send him to retrieve the Horn of Jorgen Windcaller only to discover that it was stolen by someone named Delphine, an old member of the Blades. After you retrieve the horn from her and return to the Greybeards, your training with them is done. Delphine though, asks you to help her at a dragon burial site and you witness Alduin, the dragon that attacked Helgen in the beginning, resurrecting a dragon that you then fight. After Delphine is convinced that you are the Dragonborn, she tasks you with breaking into the Thalmor embassy to see if they have anything to do with the dragons returning, they don't she's just a little prejiduce towards the Thalmor (for good reasons, most players are). You don't find anything on the Thalmor and the dragons, but you do find out that another member of the Blades, Esbern, is alive and hiding in Riften. You go find him and the Dragonborn, Delphine, and Esbern got an ancient Akaviri temple to read the prophecy of Alduin's return tp find any clues of how to defeat him. You find an ancient shout called Dragonrend that can take dragons from out of the sky. To learn this shout you go to Blackreach, a long lost underground dwarven civilization, to find an Elder Scroll and go back in time to witness Alduin's banishment. You learn the shout and once you return Alduin shows up and the two of you fight. After defeating him, Alduin goes to Sovngarde to hide. The Dragonborn captures a dragon in Dragonsreach and uses that dragon to travel to Skaldafn, an ancient nord ruin with a portal to Sovngarde. The Dragonborn travels to Sovngarde to defeat Alduin and returns to Nirn.

The Elder Scrolls V also got some DLC content in the form of the Dawnguard, Hearthfire, Dragonborn, and Creation Club. The Dawnguard DLC revolves around the return of the vampire threat. In the DLC you can side with the Dawnguard, vampire hunters, or the Volkihar vampires. No matter which side you choose the story is pretty much the same. Lord Harkon, the leader of the Volkihar wants to bring about a prophecy to blot out the sun. In this process you discover an ancient artifact called Auri-el's Bow which can be used to blot out the sun. Once you find the bow you can either give it to Lord Harkon to fulfill the prophecy or kill Lord Harkon and stop the prophecy from coming ture. The Hearthfire DLC is just a home building simulator. You can build homes and adopt children and pets. The Dragonborn DLC is based on the island of Solstheim and the return of Miraak, the dragon priest that tried to take over the dragons and rule on his own. When you first arrive to Solstheim some people are acting almost like zombies. They are being controlled by Miraak from Apocrypha, Hermeus Mora's plane of oblivion. The Dragonborn goes all throughout Solstheim in search of Black Books, items of Hermeus Mora. You eventually find the right book to take you to Miraak and you defeat him and become Hermeus Mora's new champion. The Creation Club isn't actually a DLC, but it's a microtransaction shop that can add new items into the game, some of the items being a sort of mini DLC, but nothing to game changing.

--Side Games--

The Elder Scrolls Adventures: Redguard

The Elder Scrolls Adventures: Redguard came out in 1998. The game takes place in Hammerfell on the island of Stros M'kai during 864 of the 2nd Era. You play as Cyrus, a former pirate searching for his missing sister. When Cyrus arrives in Stros M'kai, the island is in a state of politcal turmoil. Cyrus discovers that there is a plan to rebel against the Empire and gets tangled up Hammerfells fight for independence.



The Elder Scrolls Legends: Battlespire

The Scrolls Legends: Battlespire came out in 1997. The game takes place in the planes of oblivion during 398 of the 3rd Era. You play as The Apprentice and have to stop Mehrunes Dagon from ivading Nirn. You travel through multiple planes of oblivion and fight many of Mehrunes Dagon's minions.



The Elder Scrolls Online

The Elder Scrolls Online came out in 2014. The game takes place all over Tamriel during 582 of the 2nd Era. It is an online MMORPG set in the Elder Scrolls universe so you can play with friends in Tamriel. It also the earliest game timeline wise in the series.



The Elder Scrolls Legends

The Elder Scrolls Legends came out in 2017. The game was set during 175, 188, and 201 of the 4th Era. It is strategy based card game that gives lore events that transpired in between the events of Oblivion and Skyrim.



The Elder Scrolls Blades

The Elder Scrolls Blades came out in 2019. The game is set during 180 of the 4th Era. You play as The Fugitive who returns to their hometown and finds it in flames. The game centers around finding out who destroyed the town and rebuilding.



The Elder Scrolls Castles

The Elder Scrolls Castles came out in 2024. It is a mobile game where you can build and manage your own castle. You can gather resources, customize your castle, and complete quests. Very similar to Clash of Clans or Fallout Shelter.
