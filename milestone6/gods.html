--Aedra / Nine Divines--

Akatosh

Akatosh is the god of time and first of the gods of the Nine Divines. He is often depicted as a golden dragon and he is the father of all dragons. Akatosh can bless men with the blood and soul of a dragon making them Dragonborns. The first Dragonborn was named Miraak, but how he got the powers of Dragonborn isn't made clear. The entire Septim bloodline is made up of Dragonborns since Akatosh created the Amulet of Kings for Alessia and blessed her with the dragon blood. Akatosh can start events known as Dragon Breaks. Dragon Breaks are when the timeline of Nirn is split and since Akatosh is a dragon and not just the god of time, but time himself, it is seen as breaking the dragon. There have been three Dragon Breaks in recorded history, but they are hard to identify because people are usually unaware of when they happen. Akatosh is also thoght to Auri-El in the elven pantheon.



Arkay

Arkay is the god of life and death. He is often depicted an hourglass. Some accounts of Arkay's origins are that he was originally a man that ascended to godhood, but that is just speculation. Arkay's followers are usually seen in the Halls of the Dead in the cities around Tamriel, believing it is their sacred duty to protect the souls of the dead. Arkay and his followers also are fiercly against Necromancy and any forms of the undead. Arkay is also thought to be Tu'whacca in the Redgurad pantheon.



Dibella

Dibella is the goddes of beauty, love, art, and music. She is often depicted as a woman in repose. Most of her followers have a repuation practicing and worshipping Dibella in a more erotic form and it is called the Dibellan arts.



Julianos

Julianos is the god of wisdom and logic. He is often depicted as sales of balance. Julianos is most often worshipped by wizards and scholars. Julianos is also called Jhunal in the Nordic pantheon.



Kynareth

Kynareth is the goddess of air, sky, wind, and nature. She is often depicted as open sky. Kynareth is worshipped by farmers, sailors, and hunters. She is most notablly worshipped by the Greybeards though know her as Kyne. Kynareth is also called Khenarthi in the Khajiit pantheon and Tava in the Redguard pantheon.



Mara

Mara is the goddess of love and compassion. She is often depicted as paired hands. Mara and her followers all believe in love. They preach for empathy and untiy. Temples of Mara are where people go to be married. Mara is said to be the one that helped Arkay ascend to godhood. Mara is also known as Morwha in the Redguard pantheon.



Stendarr

Stendarr is the god of mercy, might, and justice. He is often depicted as a gauntlet. The most notable worshippers of Stendarr are monster hunters. The most well known organization are the Vigilants of Stendarr, a group of Stendarr's worshippers dedicated to eradicating all things evil, especially daedra. Stendarr is also known as S'rendarr in the Khajiiti pantheon and Stuhn in the nord pantheon.



Talos

Talos is the god of war and governance. Talos used to be a man named Tiber Septim, the emperor that united all of Tamriel under the Empire and founded the third Empire. He ascended to godhood after his death and was worshipped as Talos. Talos is ninth, last, and most controversial divine in the Nine. Worship of Talos was banned by the signing of the White-Gold Concordat between the Empire and the Aldmeri Dominion in the 4th Era.


--Daedra--

Azura

Azura is the daedric prince of dusk and dawn. Despite being a daedra, who are considered evil and demonic, Azura is actually considered to be the most benevolent of the daedric princes. Azura's daedric artifact is Azura's Star, a soul gem that never breaks and can capture white souls. However, in Skyrim, in Azura's daedric quest you can change the fate of Azura's Star, making it The Black Star. It's similar to Azura's star where it will never break, the difference is that The Black Star can only capture black souls, the souls of men and mer. Her other artifact is The Ring of Azura and gives the wearer night vision. Her plane of oblivion is Moonshadow, the most beautiful realm of oblivion to exist.



Boethiah

Boethiah is the daedric prince of deceit, conspiracy, and murder. Boethiah's daedric artifacts are the Ebony Mail and Goldbrand. The Ebonymail is a set of heavy armor that cloaks the wearer in shadow when sneaking and sucks the life out of those that come close to the wearer. Goldbrand is a golden, one-handed katanna that is on fire. Emperor Titus Mede II was rumored to have wielded Goldbrand in battle when taking back the Imperial City from the Aldmeri Dominion in the 4th Era. Boethiah's plane of oblivion is Attribution's Share, a land full of maze gardens and twisted towers.



Clavicus Vile

Clavicus Vile is the daeric prince of deals, pacts, and desire. The trick with Clavicus Vile is that when you make a deal with him, expect there to be a trick somewhere. Clavicus Vile's daedric artifacts are the Bitter Cup, Masque of Clavicus Vile, Rueful Axe, and Umbra Sword. The Bitter Cup is considered highly sacred by Clavicus's followers and was thought to be lost in the ruins Ald Redaynia. The Masque of Clavicus Vile was made when a disfigured noblewoman made a deal with Clavicus to make her beautiful. Clavicus gave her the mask and people began to love and admire. She eventually got married and became pregnant, but Clavicus returned and took his mask back and the noblewoman was then kicked out by her husband. The Rueful Axe was given to a man by Clavicus Vile when he asked Clavicus to help cure his daughter of lycanthropy. The Umbra Sword was a sword that Clavicus Vile asked Naenra Waerr to make and trap souls. Clavicus put some of his essence in the sword and that essence became sentient, calling itself Umbra. Anyone who wields the sword starts calling themselves Umbra, like they were taken over. Clavicus's plane of oblivion is called The Fields of Regret, a land that smells of perfume and rotting flesh.



Hermaeus Mora

Hermaeus Mora is the daedric prince of knowledge. Hermaeus Mora's daedric artifacts are the Oghma Infinium and the Black Books. The Oghma Infinium is an acient text of knowledge that is bound in skin. The Black Books are not just one artifact, but multiple. They tomes of ancient knwoledge that can transport who ever reads them to Apocrypha, where they can gain hidden knowledge and power. Hermaeus Mora's plane of oblivion is Apocrypha, a dark land full of books, black seas, and tentacles.



Hircine

Hircine is the daedric prince of the hunt, beasts, and lycanthropy. Lycanthropy is Hircine's gift he gives to his followers in which they can transform into animals like werewolves, werebears, hagravens, and weresharks among many more. Hircine's daedric artifacts are the Savior's Hide, Hircine's Ring, and the Spear of the Hunter. The Savior's Hide is a piece of light armor that gives the wearer resistance to magic and posion. Hircine's Ring is a ring that grants the wearer the ability to become a werewolf. Lycanthropes that don the ring are no longer affected by bloodlust or the moons. Hircine's plane of oblivion is The Hunting Grounds, a land full of forests where his followers that end up there become hunted or hunters.



Malacath

Malacath is the daedric prince of vengence and the ostrasized. Malacath is mainly worshipped by orcs. Malacath's daedric artifacts are Volendrung and Scourge. Volendrung is warhammer that originally belonged to a dwarven colony leader. The colony was looking to move somewhere else and the leader decided to throw Volendrung in the air and build the new colony where it lands. Volendrung landed in modern day Hammerfell, hence the name. Scourge is an ebony mace that was forged in the Fires of Fickledire. Malacath's plane of oblivion is the Ashpit, consisting of nothing but ash, dust, and smoke. There is no ground, but the orcs who stayed true to Malacath reside in the Ashen Forge, a place where drinking, feasting, and battling happen all the timeand every orc has wives.



Mehrunes Dagon

Mehrunes Dagon is the daedric prince of destruction and revolution. Mehrunes Dagon is responsible for many different attacks like the Oblivion Crisis and the Assault of Battlespire. Mehrunes Dagon's daedric artifacts are the Mysterium Xarxes and Mehrunes Razor. The Mysterium Xarxes is the holy book of the Mythic Dawn, the cult that worships Mehrunes Dagon and aided in the Oblivion Crisis. What the book actually says is unknown, but Dagon gave the book to Mankar Camoran, the leader of the Mythic Dawn and possible Dragonborn. Mehrunes Razor is a dagger that has the ability to kill anything instantly and it is praised among assassins. Mehrunes Dagon's plane of oblivion is the Deadlands, a land full of lava lakes, pouches made of flesh, and plants that try to kill you.



Mephala

Mephala is the daedric prince of secrets, plots, assassination, sex, and murder. Mephala is the lead patron of the Morag Tong, an assassination guild that operates in Morrowind. Some say that Mephala is also secretly the Night Mother, one of the beings worshipped by the Dark Brotherhood. Mephala's daedric artifacts are the Ebony Blade and the Ring of Khajiit. The Ebony Blade is a two-handed katanna that grows increasingly more powerful everytime its wielder kills someone close to them. The Ring of Khajiit is a ring used a lot by thieves because of its ability to make the wearer invisible. Mephala's plane of oblivion is The Spiral Skein, a land held together almost like a web, centered around The Pillar Palace of Mephala, though that's not its actual name.



Meridia

Meridia is the daedric prince of light and morality. Meridia is also considered one of the more benevolent daedra, much like Azura. Meridia is also extremely against the undead, she hates them with a passion. Meridia's only daedric artifact is Dawnbreaker, a one-handed sword infused with light, setting anyone hit by it on fire, and if it used to kill undead, they explode. Meridia's plane of oblivion is the Colored Rooms, not one plane, but a collection belonging to Meridia where little is actually known about them.



Molag Bal

Molag Bal is the daedric prince of domination and slavery. Molag Bal has the power of turning mortals into vampires. Molag Bal's only daedric artifact is the Mace of Molag Bal, a demonic weapon with a depiction of Bal's face on it. Molag Bal's plane of oblivion is Coldharbour, said to be an exact copy of Nirn, but in ruins. The sky is said to be on fire and the air freezing.



Namira

Namira is the daedric prince of decay, darkness, and repulsion. Namira's followers all participate in cannibalism and Namira loves it when her followers do so. Namira's only daedric artifact is the Ring of Namira, a ring that grants the wearer a boost to their vitality when they consume flesh. Namira's plane of oblivion is the Scuttling Void and nothing is really known about it because any men or mer that verntured there never came back.



Nocturnal

Nocturnal is the daedric prince of night, darkness, and shadow, and patron of all thieves. Nocturnal's daedric artifacts are the Bow of Shadows, the Eye of Nocturnal, Gray Cowl of Nocturnal, and the Skeleton Key. The Bow of Shadows is a bow that grants its user invisibilty and increased speed. The Eye of Nocturnal is an artifact that Nocturnal uses to see into the mortal plane. The Gray Cowl of Nocturnal was a cowl owned by Nocturnal that was stolen by a thief and she cursed it so anyone that wears it, their identity is erased from history. The cowl is worn by the leader of the Thieves Guild called the Gray Fox. The Skeleton Key is an unbreakable lockpick owned by Nocturnal that is dreamed of by all thieves. Nocturnal's plane of oblivion is the Evergloam, a land covered in darkness and fog.



Peryite

Peryite is the daedric prince of disease and pestilence. Peryite has the appearance of a dragon, but he is considered one of the weakest daedra. Peryite's only daedric artifact is Spell Breaker, a dwarven shield that casts a ward, protecting the user from all incoming spells. Peryite's plane of oblivion is The Pits and little is known about it because it is unreachable by mortal means and the only beings to visit it are other daedra.



Sanguine

Sanguine is the daedric prince of hedonism, debauchery, and indulgence. Sanguine likes to play pranks on mortals and pressure them into sinful lifestyles. Sanguine's only daedric artifact is Sanguine Rose, a staff that looks like a rose picked straight from Sanguine's own plane of oblivion and it summons a daedra to help the user. Sanguine's plane of oblivion is the Myriad Realms of Revelry, which is actually multiple realms that can be customzed to meet any needs and desires of the occupant.



Sheogorath

Sheogorath is the daedric prince of madness. Sheogorath is actually one half of a daedra, the other being Jyggalag. An event called the Greymarch occurs every now and then where Sheogorath becomes Jyggalag and destroys Sheo's plane of oblivion over and over again. This loop is ended during the events of The Elder Scrolls IV: Oblivion Shivering Isles DLC, in which the Hero of Kvatch helps stop the Greymarch and Jyggalag and literally becomes the new Sheogorath. Sheogorath has many daedric artifacts. They are the Wabbajack, Staff of the Everscamp, Fork of Horripilation, Spear of Bitter Mercy, Staff of Sheogorath, Gambolpuddy, and Follum Discognitum. The Wabbajack is a staff that when used has random things happen to whoever it is used against. This can range from turning someone into a block of cheese to summoning an angry dremora that attacks you. The Staff of the Everscamp summons four everscamps and if one is killed, a new one takes its place, but only for a short amount of time. The staff also can't be dropped or sold and can only be gotten rid of if you the wielder gives it someone who is willing to take it. The Fork of Horripilation is literally just a fork that drains someones magicka. The Spear of Bitter Mercy is able to reflect magical attacks back at the attacker and can summon a storm atronach. The Staff of Sheogorath has the ability to freeze opponents in a certain range. Sheogorath's voive booms from the staff when activated saying "Hold!" to freeze everyone and "Release!" when the spell wears off. Gambolpuddy is a right glove that when worn fortifies half of the wearers skills while also draining the rest. Folium Discognitum is book that is full of the insigths of people who have gone insane and can give the reader immense knowledge. Sheogorath's plane of oblivion is the Shivering Isles. It is whatever Sheogorath wills it to be.



Jyggalag

Jyggalag is the daedric prince of order. Jyggalag used to be the most powerful daedric prince until the other daedra began to fear him and his power. The other princes cursed Jyggalag to turn into Sheogorath, the complete opposite of everything he stands for. At the end of the Shivering Isles DLC and the cycle of Greymarch is ended, Jyggalag is set free and he can now roam the planes of oblivion again. Jyggalag has only one daedric artifact, the Sword of Jyggalag, a powerful two-handed sword that belongs to Jyggalag. Jyggalag's plane of oblivion is technically the Shivering Isles, but because of his split personality he doesn't really get the chance to be in it.



Vaermina

Vaermina is the daedric prince of dreams and nightmares. Her only daedric artifact is the Skull of Corruption, a staff that steals the dreams of sleeping people and replaces them with nightmares. Vaermina's plane of oblivion is the Quagmire, a land that changes its look into something newly horrifying everytime lightning strikes.
