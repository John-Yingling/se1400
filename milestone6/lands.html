--Tamriel--

Tamriel is the continent all the Elder Scrolls games takes place on. There are a total of ten provinces in Tamriel. High Rock, Hammerfell, Skyrim, Morrowind, Cyrodiil, Summerset Isles, Valenwood, Elsweyr, and Black Marsh.


High Rock

High Rock is the north-western province of Tamriel. The capital city is Daggerfall, the largest and southern most city in High Rock. The terrain of High Rock veries from place to place. There are coastal hills, forests, windy crags, and mountain ranges. High Rock borders Hammerfell to its southeast and skyrim to its east. The main inhabitants of High Rock are the Bretons.



Hammerfell

Hammerfell is the western province of Tamriel. The capital city is Sentinel, a massive port city with heavy navy presence and busy markets. The terrain in Hammerfell is deserts and rocky highlands with few coastal cities like Sentinel. The deserts in Hammerfell are full of massive sandstorms, dwarven ruins, bandits, and nomadic tribes. The Dragontail Mountains are in the north of Hammerfell and act as a border from other provinces. The montains have many strongholds and mysteries. Hammerfell borders High Rock to its north, Skyrim to its northeast, and Cyrodiil to its east. The main inhabitants of Hammerfell are the Redguards.



Skyrim

Skyrim is the northern province of Tamriel. The capital city is Solitude, a rich and refined city that also houses the empires main base of operations for Skyrim. The terrain of Skyrim is very harsh. The entire province is snowy tundra, icy coastlines, mountains, and forests full of bandits, ancient nord ruins, dwarven ruins, and old forts. If the draugr of the nord ruins don't scare you, the falmer will. Skyrim borders High Rock to the west, Hammerfell to the southwest, Cyrodiil to south, and Morrowind to east. The main inhabitants of Skyrim are the Nords.



Morrowind

Morrowind is the northeastern province of Tamriel. The capital city is Mournhold, the polical hot spot for Morrowind and houses multiple Tribunual temples. The terrain of Morrowind is alien-like. The province has swamps, forest made up of giant mushrooms, volcanic highlands, and ash covered wastelands. The land used to be terrorized by cliff racers, but they were wiped out by Saint Jiub. Instead the Reavers plunder the land. Morrowind borders Skyrim to the northwest, Cyrodiil to west, and Black Marsh to the south. The main inhabitants of Morrowind are the Dunmer, or Dark Elves.



Cyrodiil

Cyrodiil is the central province of Tamriel. The capital city is the Imperial City, a massive city made of white stone and home of the Empire, surrounded by Lake Rumare. Cyrodiil is probably the nicest province when it comes to climage and geography. Cyrodiil has swamps to their south, snow covered mountain ranges to the north, and the rest is lush forests and beautiful fields, if your not attacked by goblins or trolls. Cyrodiil borders Hammerfell to the west, Skyrim to the north, Morrowind to the east, Black Marsh to the southeast, and Valenwood and Elsweyr to the south. The main inhabitants of Cyrodiil are the Imperials.



Summerset Isles

The Summerset Isles is an island province off the southwest coast of Tamriel. The capital city is Alinor, a shining city full of towers and light. The terrain of the Summer Isles is absolutely gorgeous. Golden beaches, crystal waters, coral cliffs, meadows, and strange flora. This would be the perfect summer vacation spot, almost ideal if it weren't for severe racism towards literally every other race by the local population. The Summerset Isles are bordered by the Etheric Ocean and the Abecean Sea. The main inhabitants of the Summerset Isles are the Altmer, or High Elves.



Valenwood

Valenwood is one of the southern provinces of Tamriel. The capital city is Falinesti, a city built a massive living tree that actually moves around the province. The terrain of Valenwood is very dangerous. The province is full thick forests, jungles, groves, and as discussed, tree cities. The trees are not the only thing living as there are, talking vines as well as many carniverous plants. If the local fauna doesn't kill or eat you, the local populous might, depending on how you treat them and their nature. Valenwood borders Cyrodiil to the north and Elsweyr to the east. The main inhabitants of Valenwood are the Bosmer, or Wood Elves.



Elsweyr

Elsweyr is one of the southern provinces of Tamriel. The capital city is Rimmen, an oasis in the northern region of Elsweyr. The terrain of Elsweyr is a 50/50 of desert and jungle. The northern half of Elsweyr is desert full of sandstorms, sand dunes, and ancient ruins. The southern half of Elsweyr is dense jungle and coasts. If the beasts of the deserts don't kill you, the ancient spirits will. Elsweyr is bordered by Valenwood to the west, Cyrodiil to the north, and Black Marsh to the east. The main inhabitants of Elsweyr are the Khajiits.



Black Marsh

Black Marsh is the southeastern province of Tamriel. Black Marsh doesn't have a true capital city, but the key cities are Lilmoth, Stormhold, and Archon. The terrain of Black Marsh is moist. The entire province is hot, humid, thick jungles, and marshes, hence the name. All the beasts there want to kill you, from the fleshflies, it's exactly as it sounds, to the werecrocodiles, yes people that transform into crocodiles, pretty much nothing is safe. Black Marsh is bordered by Elsweyr to the west, Cyrodiil to the northwest, and Morrowind to the north. The main inhabitants of Black Marsh are the Argonians


--Akavir--

Akavir is the continent east of Tamriel. Not much is known about Akavir and the only things that are known are rumor and legend. We do know that Akavir are where the dragons are originally from before they came to Tamriel with the men of Akavir, the Akaviri. There are also accounts of monkey people, vampiric snake-men, tiger-dragon people, and snow demons. This is all that is really known about Akavir.


--Atmora--

Atmora is the continent north of Tamriel. It is said to be the birth place of man and it's climate was harsh cold constantly. The terrain was said to be mountains and ancient forests, all covered in snow. Atmora was said to be at constant war with itself. After Ysgramor went back to Tamriel with his 500 companions, Atmora was rumored to have frozen over and no one knows why. Expededtions and adventures have been sent to Atmora, but none have returned.


--Pyandonea--

Pyandonea is the continent south of Tamriel and essentially the Atlantis of the Elder Scrolls. Pyandonea is home to the Maomer, or Sea Elves. The continent is entirely cloaked in myth and is surrounded by deadly, storm-ridden seas, so ships that try and sail there disappear a lot. The Maomer are very similar to the Altmer, descendants of the same people and extreme hatred towards any race that isn't them. They have actually tired to invade Tamriel multiple times.


--Yokuda--

Yokuda is the continent west of Tamriel. The continent was mountainous, desert, and rocky coasts. Yokuda is now sunken into the ocean and mostly destroyed, but there a few islands that still stand. The Yokudans sailed to Tamriel and settled in Hammerfell. They are now known as the Redguards.
